My name is Justin Tsai and I am currently a sophmore attending Dublin High school. I enjoy biology and the life sciences, so I hope to one day become a general physician. Becoming a doctor has been my goal for a long time because my mother, who is a dentist, has inspired me to also go into the medical field. In order to achieve this goal, I often volunteer my time at medical clinics or my mom's office to get some more experience and to see how the professionals operate. The health field has always fascinated me and I hope to employ some of the coding skills that I have learned in my computer science engineering class to my future job. In the future, I hope to attend UCLA as my undergraduate school and UCSF as a graduate school for my professional degree. To reach my goals, I must be diligent and continue to work hard throughout high school to maintain my grades. Academics are very important to me and I always prioritize schoolwork above everything else.

One of the most prominent characteristics of myself is that I have perseverance. When things are tough, I always find a way to push through and endure. This skill is what has allowed me to be an active athlete who practices for over 30 hours a week and a student who maintains a respectable grade point average. In addition to swimming outside of school, I also play the piano, clarinet, and violin because I have a passion for music. I work as a part time life guard in Pleasanton at the Dolores Bengston Aquatics Center for the last year. Doing lifeguarding has taught me to be responsible and that my consequences have actions because if someone is not vigilant when they are on duty, then a multitude of disasters can happen. When I am not working or trying to better myself, I enjoy relaxing at home with my two sisters, Emily and Sabrina. They look up to me as a role model and we have been playing video games or basketball with each other since childhood. As a big brother, I do my best to protect my little sisters and teach them the difference between right and wrong.

I am also a member of the Dublin High Swimming Team and went to the North Coast Section competition in my first year on the team. Being on the team has brought me closer to many of my fellow students and I hope to continue to make friends when the sophmore season starts. One of my dreams is also to be a captain of the swim team because I want to be able to represent my school and lead my fellow students to victory. Although taking a position of leadership is not easy, I want to challenge myself to reach greater heights and push myself out of my comfort zone. I believe that self motivation and self improvement are necessary skills of people who are successful. Other than participating in school sports, I also am a member of the KEY club which volunteers their time at centers for those who are less fortunate. In the future, perhaps in my junior year, I will start a club of my own to help the less fortunate.
